Top 10 Fantasy Cricket Strategies to Dominate IPL 2025

June 22, 2025 6 min read By DDOB Expert Team 12,400 views
Home Blog Top 10 Fantasy Cricket Strategies

IPL 2025 is here, and with over ₹500 Crore in total fantasy prize money on the line, the difference between winning and losing often comes down to one or two tactical decisions. After analysing more than 10,000 winning teams from top-ranked players on Devansh DOB Online Book, we've distilled the exact strategies that separate consistent winners from the rest.

Whether you're a beginner building your first team or an experienced player looking to fine-tune your approach, these 10 proven strategies will give you a serious edge in every contest.

Strategy #1–5: The Fundamentals

1

Always Research the Pitch Report

The pitch is the single most overlooked factor in fantasy cricket. A spinning Chepauk pitch means you should load up on spinners and batters who play spin well. A flat Wankhede track? Pick pacers and big-hitting batters. Check the last 5 matches played at the venue — the data never lies.

2

Captain & Vice-Captain Are 50% of Your Score

Your Captain earns 2x points and your Vice-Captain earns 1.5x. A correct captain pick on a player who scores 80+ runs or takes 3+ wickets can single-handedly win your contest. Never go with the "safe" popular captain in mega contests — you need a differentiator.

3

Pick 1–2 Differential Players Per Team

In head-to-head contests, having 90% team overlap means you win and lose together. Include at least one player with under 20% selection rate who has a strong matchup. This is how players go from average to top 1% finishes in big tournaments.

4

Check the Toss Before Finalising Your Team

At venues where chasing teams historically win more (e.g., Kolkata), batting-order positions shift dramatically after the toss. If the toss hasn't happened, wait. Our platform allows team edits up to 5 minutes before the match — use every second.

5

Don't Pick Injured or Resting Players

This sounds obvious, but thousands of players lose contests every week by picking players who don't feature in the playing XI. Check official team announcements and trusted sources within 1 hour of match start. Never rely on last week's team sheet.

Pro Tip

Create multiple teams for the same match. One aggressive team with a differential captain for the grand prize, and one conservative team with the popular captain for smaller head-to-head contests. Diversifying your entries is how professionals manage risk.

Strategy #6–10: Advanced Tactics

6

Prioritise All-Rounders in Smaller Leagues

All-rounders offer double contribution points — batting AND bowling. In leagues where every point matters, a top-order all-rounder like Hardik Pandya or Axar Patel who bats at 4 and bowls 4 overs can outscore a specialist batter who only has one chance to contribute.

7

Track Form, Not Reputation

MS Dhoni may be a legend, but if he hasn't scored above 25 in his last 8 innings, picking him for a batting-heavy strategy is emotional, not tactical. Use last 5-match averages, not career averages. Current form beats big names in fantasy every time.

8

Exploit Powerplay & Death Overs Specialists

Powerplay openers and death-over bowlers accumulate points faster than middle-order batters. A bowler who consistently takes wickets in overs 18–20, or an opener who scores 30–40 runs in 15 balls, earns more fantasy points per ball faced than most other positions.

9

Set a Budget and Stick to It Across Entries

Each team has a ₹100 credit cap. Don't overspend on 2–3 premium players and fill the rest with poor-form cheap options. The sweet spot is 3–4 high-value players (₹9–11 credits each) and 7 mid-range players (₹7–9 credits) who are in form. Balance beats star power in a 100-credit system.

10

Review Your Performance After Every Match

The players who consistently rank in the top 10% do one thing others don't: they review every team after each match. What worked? What was the captain mistake? Did the pitch play differently than expected? 10 minutes of post-match analysis is worth 10x more than pre-match guessing.

"The goal isn't to pick the best XI — it's to pick the best XI that nobody else has selected. That's how you win ₹10 Lakh contests, not by picking the obvious team."
— DDOB Expert Team

Final Thoughts

Fantasy cricket rewards research, patience, and discipline — not luck. Apply these 10 strategies consistently over an IPL season and you will see measurable improvement in your contest rankings and winnings.

The key mindset shift: stop thinking about who you want to pick, and start thinking about who gives your team the best mathematical edge. That's the difference between a fan making a wish and a champion making a strategic decision.

  • Research pitch and weather before every team selection
  • Captain selection is the single highest-leverage decision you make
  • Differentiate in mega contests; play safe in small head-to-heads
  • Track recent form over career reputation
  • Review, learn, and improve after every single match
